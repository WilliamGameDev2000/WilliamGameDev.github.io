Catpitalism is a small-scale project made in Godot 4.1 for Global Game Jam 2024

The Game


Catpitalism is a story about David the cat trying to climb his way up the corporate ladder in an oppressive office full of...Mice! Fight your way through office floors, break rooms, and more (coming later) while taking on your managers and bosses until you reach the top.

There are a few weapons to chose from including environmental objects that can be picked up and thrown at enemies and bosses. There is currently a basic enemy type that closes in on the player (a bit like how the VampireSurvivors gameplay loop is), as well as a boss unit that has special attack patterns and projectiles.

After the submission I have also been tweaking and updating features (such as adding in the throwable objects and creating the temporary player UI) as well as polishing features to account for edgecasing and making systems more generic to help in further development.

Development & Engine


The game was made in Godot, using the in-built GDScript language which has several commonalities with Python such as forced indentation. It was a first time using GDScript for me however I was able to work it out and contribute key aspects such as the player movement system and help others with their systems as well. It was not, however, my first time using the Godot engine and it's interface and I was confident in navigation the engine and it's features and utilising them.
I also helped out by taking advantage of some of Godot's in-built functionality to help other team members with their development like finding the way to add collision to the tile-maps themselves for the level designers without needing to create a collision around the whole level piece by piece. I also helped out in debugging and troubleshooting systems during their development and after merge conflicts with the Git repo.

Mechanics


- WASD & arrow keys are movement, and SPACE performs a semi invunerable combat roll for avoiding projectiles. Movement is snappy and responsive, rather than slippery while still being momentum based. - LMB shoots projectiles from the bullet pool and throws a held object towards the mouse location. These both apply damage to any enemies they collide with. - E key picks up throwable objects when in range, they'll tint shift a light blue colour to indicate they can be held. This puts the player in an altered state where they slow down their movement speed and have to throw the object again before being able to fire their weapon again. Also interacts with certain environmental features such as containers that drop healing items before going on a short cool down. - Weapon swapping can be done at any weapon pick-up (Currently a place holder orb sprite) and will automatically change the player's weapon to the corrosponding weapon on the pick up object. Weapons can be set up with unique attributes such as their projectile's damage values, how much durability they have etc. - Healing fills up your current health bar if you're not at max for the current life. The player has nine lives that they can take three points of damage on each before not being able to heal to the next life up again. Healing is done by picking up David's favourite food item - a sandwich.
