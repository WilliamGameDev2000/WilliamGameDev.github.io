Catpitalism is a small-scale project made in Godot 4.1 for Global Game Jam 2024

The Game


Catpitalism is a story about David the cat trying to climb his way up the corporate ladder in an oppressive office full of...Mice! Fight your way through office floors, break rooms, and more (coming later) while taking on your managers and bosses until you reach the top.

There are a few weapons to chose from including environmental objects that can be picked up and thrown at enemies and bosses. There is currently a basic enemy type that closes in on the player (a bit like how the VampireSurvivors gameplay loop is), as well as a boss unit that has special attack patterns and projectiles.

After the submission I have also been tweaking and updating features (such as adding in the throwable objects) as well as polishing features to account for edgecasing and making systems more generic to help in further development.

Development


The game was made in Godot, using the in-built GDScript language which has several commonalities with Python such as forced indentation. It was a first time using GDScript for me however I was able to work it out and contribute key aspects such as the player movement system and help others with their systems as well.
I also helped out by taking advantage of some of Godot's in-built functionality to help other team members with their development like finding the way to add collision to the tile-maps intrinsically for the level designers. I also helped in debugging and troubleshooting systems during their development and after merge conflicts with the Git repo.
